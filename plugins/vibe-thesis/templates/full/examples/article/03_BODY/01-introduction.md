# Introduction

Document infrastructure shapes argument in ways the writing-process literature has not adequately mapped [@knuth1984; @lamport1986]. This article presents three case studies from the humanities that demonstrate how citation regime, sectional structure, and visual apparatus jointly constrain or enable specific argumentative moves.

The article is organized as follows. Section 2 presents the case studies and the analytical findings. We close with a discussion of generalizable claims and their limits.

Our contribution is descriptive: we do not propose a new theory of academic writing. We propose a way of *seeing* writing infrastructure as itself argumentative — and we work through what falls out of that lens in three concrete cases.
