# Results

## Empirical Findings

Across the 47 works in our corpus, infrastructure decisions clustered in patterns that surprised us. We expected fields to drive convergence — and they did — but the within-field variance was larger than the cross-field variance for two of our four dimensions. Citation regime varied substantially within fields; sectional structure varied less.

The clustering is visible in figures 1 and 2 (omitted from this fixture). The visualizations were generated using the methodology described in chapter 2.

## Discussion of Empirical Patterns

The within-field variance suggests authors retain more agency over infrastructure decisions than the field-conventions story would imply. We treat this as a finding rather than a noise floor, though we acknowledge the alternative interpretation: that we're seeing measurement noise from our coding scheme rather than substantive variation.
