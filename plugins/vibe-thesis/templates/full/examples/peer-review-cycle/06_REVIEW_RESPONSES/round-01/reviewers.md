# Reviewer Responses — Round 1

## Summary of changes

We thank the reviewers for their thoughtful comments. The revised manuscript addresses the major concerns raised by all three reviewers. Principal changes: (1) added explicit engagement with the conventionalist counter-position in §3 (per Reviewer 1); (2) clarified the methodological inference chain in §2 (per Reviewer 2); (3) tightened the scope claims in §1 to avoid the over-generalization Reviewer 3 flagged. Detailed point-by-point responses follow.

---

## Reviewer 1

### Comment 1.1

> The article's central claim — that document infrastructure is "argumentative" — is provocative but underspecified. What does "argumentative" mean here? The authors gesture at the idea but never define it operationally.

**Response:**

We agree this needed to be tighter. We've added a definition in §1 (paragraph 3): infrastructure is "argumentative" when the choice constrains or enables specific argumentative moves — making some kinds of claims feel natural to construct and others feel forced. This operationalizes the term in a way the case studies can directly demonstrate.

**Change made:** §1, paragraph 3, lines 18–24.

### Comment 1.2

> The conventionalist counter-position deserves direct engagement. The authors bypass it.

**Response:**

Fair. We've added §3.4 ("The Conventionalist Objection") which engages directly with the position that infrastructure is inherited rather than chosen. Our response: even when conventions dominate, *which* conventions an author submits to is itself a choice with argumentative consequences — and our case studies show authors who chose differently within their fields.

**Change made:** New §3.4, ~600 words.

### Comment 1.3

> The case study selection feels convenient. How were these three chosen?

**Response:**

We thank the reviewer for this observation. The selection criteria are explicit in §2.1; we've expanded the discussion to acknowledge the convenience-sampling concern directly and to note that the cases were not chosen to confirm the thesis but to span the four infrastructure dimensions the framework identifies.

**Change made:** §2.1, paragraph 4, lines 67–82.

## Reviewer 2

### Comment 2.1

> The methodological inference chain in §2.3 ("Effect Attribution") is the article's weakest point. The three strategies (testimony, contrast, reconstruction) carry different evidentiary weight, but the article uses them interchangeably.

**Response:**

The reviewer is right. We've restructured §2.3 to (a) explicitly rank the three strategies by evidentiary weight (testimony > contrast > reconstruction), (b) flag in each case study which strategy supports each inference, and (c) note where reconstruction is doing more work than testimony or contrast.

**Change made:** §2.3, full restructure (~400 words rewritten); each case study in §3 now includes inline strategy labels.

### Comment 2.2

> Coding by two readers is good. Inter-rater agreement statistics?

**Response:**

We thank the reviewer for catching this gap. Inter-rater agreement (Cohen's κ) ranges from 0.71 (genre conformity dimension) to 0.84 (citation regime dimension). We've added a table to §2.2 reporting per-dimension agreement and discussing the dimensions where disagreement was higher.

**Change made:** §2.2, new Table 1 (inter-rater agreement); paragraph added discussing the genre-conformity dimension's lower agreement.

## Reviewer 3

Agreed and corrected. We've replaced "academic writing" with "humanities and humanistic social sciences scholarship" or similar field-specific phrasing throughout §1 and §4. We've also added an explicit scope note in §1 acknowledging that natural-sciences writing differs and falls outside our analysis.

**Change made:** §1 (multiple lines: 5, 12, 28, 34); §4, new opening paragraph.

### Comment 3.2

> The conclusion's claims need similar scope-tightening.

**Response:**

We thank the reviewer for this observation. No change made because the §1 scope note is referenced throughout §4 to bound the conclusion's claims; we believe the §4 claims are now appropriately scoped given the §1 boundary. If the reviewer believes specific §4 sentences still over-claim, we'd appreciate pointers to those sentences for further revision.
