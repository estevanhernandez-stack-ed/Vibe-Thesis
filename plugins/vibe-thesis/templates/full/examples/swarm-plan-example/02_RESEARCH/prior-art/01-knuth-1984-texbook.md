# Knuth, *The TeXbook* (1984)

**Citation:** Knuth, Donald E. *The TeXbook.* Reading, MA: Addison-Wesley, 1984.

## One-paragraph summary

Knuth's manual for his typesetting system TeX. Beyond technical documentation, it articulates a stance toward typographic decisions as authorial decisions — the typesetter is not separable from the author, and visual choices carry argumentative weight in technical writing. The system's design embodies the claim: TeX exposes typesetting decisions to authorial control rather than hiding them behind word-processor defaults.

## Main claim

Typographic deliberation is part of authorship, not a downstream production concern. A typesetting system that hides decisions from the author cedes authorial control over how the argument lands.

## Connection to thesis

Strong support for the infrastructure-is-argumentative thesis. Knuth provides a foundational example: the design of TeX itself enacts the claim that infrastructure is choice, not neutral substrate. The book is a primary source for the technical-writing wing of our argument.
