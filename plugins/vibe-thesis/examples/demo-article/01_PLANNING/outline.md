# Outline — A Brief Examination of Rubber Ducks in Software Engineering

## §1. Introduction

The practice. The lore. Where it appears in the literature [@hunt1999pragmatic].
The puzzle: why does explaining to an inert object work?

## §2. Methods — the duck protocol

What the practice actually looks like in the field. Two postures: line-by-line
explanation and goal-explanation. Conditions under which the duck helps
[@brooks1995mythical]. Conditions under which the duck doesn't. The role of
articulation in surfacing implicit theory [@naur1985programming].

## §3. Conclusion

A small taxonomy: the duck as cognitive scaffold (fits [@norman1988design]),
the duck as audience-of-one, the duck as commitment device. What makes a duck
worth talking to is not the duck — it is the requirement to articulate.
Teaching one to debug, then, is teaching the articulation, with or without a
duck. Brief nod to literate programming [@knuth1984literate] as the same
discipline, written down.
